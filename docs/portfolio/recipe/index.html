Tartiflette Recipe

Posted on: 1/1/2024

The Ultimate Comfort Food

The Tartiflette is a traditional dish from the Savoy region in the French Alps. It is made with potatoes, Reblochon cheese, lardons, and onions. Perfect for a cold winter day!

  • Potatoes: At least 8 medium-sized
  • Onions: 2 small
  • Cheese: 2 half-slices of Reblochon (or 1 whole Reblochon cut horizontally)
  • Meat: 1 package of lardons (bacon strips/cubes)
  • Seasoning:
    • 1 clove of garlic
    • 2 tbsp olive oil
    • Salt & Pepper
    • Oregano & Herbes de Provence

1. Prep the potatoes

Place the potatoes in a large pot of water. Bring to a boil and cook on low heat for 15 minutes. Tip: Do not fully cook them, they will finish cooking in the oven.

2. Cook the garnish

Meanwhile, roughly chop the onions. Saut√© them in a frying pan over low heat with the olive oil until they are soft. In the same pan (or separately), cook the lardons until browned.

3. Assembly

  • Drain the potatoes, peel them, and cut them into rounds (slices).
  • Preheat your oven to 200¬∞C (400¬∞F).
  • Peel the garlic clove and rub it vigorously against the inside of your gratin dish (this adds a subtle flavor).

4. Layering

In the gratin dish, create layers in the following order:

  1. A layer of potatoes
  2. A layer of onions
  3. A layer of lardons
  4. Another layer of potatoes

5. The Cheese

Finish by placing the Reblochon cheese on top (crust side up usually implies crisper result, but follow your preference). Cut the pieces if necessary to ensure the cheese covers the whole dish.

6. Baking

Bake for approximately 20 minutes until the cheese is melted and golden brown.

Note: Serve with a fresh green salad and a glass of white wine from Savoy!
